RL from Physical Feedback: Aligning Large Motion Models with Humanoid Whole-Body Control

Abstract

Text-to-motion generation builds a bridge between text instructions and humanoid robot actions. Existing studies have primarily focused on the semantic correspondence of generated motions. However, in practice, generated motions from these methods often suffer from significant physical inconsistency, hindering their deployment in real humanoid robots. To address this issue, we propose a novel reinforcement learning framework–Reinforcement Learning from Physical Feedback (RLPF). Our approach leverages a pre-trained motion tracking policy to assess the feasibility of generated motions in a physics simulator. Additionally, it incorporates an alignment verification module to guide the generation of motions that closely adhere to the input instructions. We then fine-tune the large motion model to jointly optimize these two objectives via reinforcement learning. Experimental results demonstrate that RLPF greatly enhances the physical feasibility of motions generated by the large motion model while maintaining the semantic correspondence.

RLPF



[Overview of the RLPF framework] Our training framework, RLPF, consists of three key components:
  • Motion Tracking Policy, which is pretrained to establish a motion tracking reward to evaluate generated motions;
  • Alignment Verification Module, which enhances text-motion semantic consistency while preserving physical feasibility;
  • RL Optimization Framework that jointly optimizes the physical feasibility and semantic alignment of motions generated by the large motion model.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the proposed Reinforcement Learning from Physical Feedback (RLPF) framework effectively addresses the challenge of physical inconsistency in large motion models for humanoid robots. By integrating a pre-trained motion tracking policy and an alignment verification module, RLPF ensures that generated motions are both physically feasible and semantically correspond with input instructions. Experimental results confirm that fine-tuning the large motion model with reinforcement learning greatly enhances the physical feasibility of motions generated by the large motion model while maintaining the semantic correspondence.
